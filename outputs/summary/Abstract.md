
Project Title: Predicting Youth Violence and Weapon Behaviors Through School Safety Concerns and Layered Contexts

This statistical research project investigates the predictive relationship between school-induced fear (measured by days skipped due to safety concerns) and adolescent defensive violence behaviors. Using the 2007 National YRBS dataset, the analysis employs an Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) Multiple Linear Regression model, effectively controlling for biological sex and underlying depressive trauma.

Key Findings:
1. A direct dose-response relationship exists: as perceived environmental danger escalates, students resort to defensive arming and physical fighting in a near-linear fashion.
2. Male students exhibit a drastically steeper escalation slope in violence risk under fear compared to their female peers.
3. A "perfect storm" of vulnerabilities occurs when a student experiences both environmental fear and internal psychological distress (depression), multiplying the risk of lethal behavior.

Conclusion:
School violence is not purely unprovoked; it is strongly correlated with a "survival mode" triggered by perceived systemic insecurity. Ensuring students' physical and psychological safety is the most effective preventive measure to disarm youth.

專案名稱：校園安全疑慮對青少年暴力與武器行為之預測及深層影響

本統計研究專案旨在探討校園不安全感（以因安全疑慮而缺課的天數衡量）與青少年防禦性暴力行為之間的預測關係。分析過程使用 2007 年全美 YRBS 數據集，並採用最小平方法（OLS）多變數線性迴歸模型，精準控制了生理性別與潛在的憂鬱創傷因素。

核心發現：
1. 存在直接的「劑量-反應」關係：當感知的環境危險升級時，學生會以近乎線性的方式訴諸防禦性武裝與肢體衝突。
2. 應對機制的性別分歧：在面臨恐懼時，男學生在暴力風險上的升級斜率比女學生陡峭許多。
3. 脆弱性疊加的「完美風暴」：當學生同時面臨環境恐懼與內在心理困擾（憂鬱）時，會引發嚴重的行為失衡，使致命行為的風險呈倍數增長。

結論：
校園暴力並非純粹的無端攻擊，它與因環境不安全感所引發的「求生模式」高度相關。確保學生在生理與心理上的絕對安全，才是預防青少年武裝自我與暴力行為最有效的根本措施。
